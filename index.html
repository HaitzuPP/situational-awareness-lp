The Thesis
Aschenbrenner exited Nvidia and Broadcom in Q4 2025. He is betting on the physical infrastructure the AI buildout requires: electricity, data center capacity, and optical networking. Bloom Energy (the flagship) generates power directly at the data center using solid oxide fuel cells, bypassing the overloaded grid. The rest of the book extends that logic: Bitcoin miners with cheap stranded power pivoting to AI/HPC hosting, natural gas producers supplying baseload power, and optical component makers carrying AI traffic between GPUs. The fund scaled from $225M to $5.5B in under a year on this single infrastructure thesis.
